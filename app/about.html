About Me

I came to Chiapas from Berkeley, California in May of 2008 to build experimental software for a small research University, El Colegio de La Frontera Sur (ECOSUR). My soon to be wife and I drove across California, Arizona, New Mexico and Texas to spend our last day as U.S. residents at the border, in Laredo, Texas. We then entered Mexico through Nuevo Laredo and made it to the colonial town of San Cristóbal de las casas after less than a week's driving. We have lived together here ever since, getting married in 2010 (in New Orleans) and building and living in a small house, close to the center, in Barrio Cerillo.

We have two dogs, a labrador and a long-haired chihuahua; along with several plants. I spend my days programming and configuring systems at ECOSUR. In the evenings, I study algorithms, play with our dogs, and generally enjoy my family.

As part of my open source work with rules based gaming systems, I have maintained a more public presence on the network by means of Twitter, Google Plus and other social systems. Please see the links in my Stream for deeper reading.

Open Source

Since 2008, most of my work has been public, open source code, hosted on GitHub. I maintain two general repositories there, one, "ecosur-sistemas-complejos" that contains the complex systems work that I built in collaboration with Max Pimm and Luis García Barrios. The second is under my own name and email and contains a number of personal projects, this website, among them.

Writing

There was a time in my life when writing was all that I ever wanted to do. Short stories, poems, these filled all of my time and imagination. For quite a long time now, I've exchanged poems for code, and spend most of my creative energy in engineering solutions. Since I've come to Chiapas, I've spent more time writing, although mainly for descriptions of research work with Luis and our work in la Sepultura.
