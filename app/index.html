About Me (a few words)

I'm back on Bainbridge Island these days, building software for a large company out of San Francisco. I work from home several days a week and commute into our office by foot for the remainder. I strive to be active in the local community and work on integrating what I learned about workshopping in Southern Mexico back into my life in the Pacific Northwest.

From 2008 - 2013, I lived in Chiapas, Mexico, building experimental software for a small research University, El Colegio de La Frontera Sur (ECOSUR). As part of my open source work with rules based gaming systems, I maintained a more public presence on the network by means of Twitter, Google Plus and other social systems. Please see the links in my Stream for more information.

Open Source (mad science)

From 2008 until 2013, most of my work was public, open source code, hosted on GitHub. I store that work in two repositories there; "ecosur-sistemas-complejos," which contains the complex systems work that I built in collaboration with Max Pimm and Luis García Barrios; the other, "ecosur-infonomía", which contains web projects built for the Infonomía (Infonomy) group. As part of my local volunteer work with Bainbridge Nodeschool, I also participate in the IslandJS team repository, which is linked to and referenced from the bottom of our group's webpage. Finally, I keep a personal workspace under "awaterma" on GitHub with a variety of projects, this website, and all the original source of the research work, among them. Please take a look below, to learn more about the open source projects I host and contribute to.
